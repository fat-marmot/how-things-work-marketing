What we shipped.

A running log of what's new in the app — new topics added to the library, reading-experience tweaks, fixes. Newest at the top.

iOS · current 1.4.0 Android · soon RSS available
1.4.0 April 22, 2026 Latest

Search, finally.

You can now type and find any of the ~400 topics. We built it offline-first, so it works on a plane.

  • NewFull-text search across all topics, including their footnotes and source list.
  • NewEight new Medicine topics, including how an MRI scanner sees inside you and how vaccines train the immune system.
  • ChangedTopic pages now remember your scroll position when you switch away and come back.
  • FixedDiagrams in landscape no longer clip on devices with home indicators.
  • FixedA handful of typos and one mis-stated unit in the solar panels piece. Thanks Ravi.
1.3.2 March 14, 2026

Smaller, faster.

A maintenance release, mostly under the hood, that shaves about 18 MB off the install size.

  • ChangedRe-encoded all diagram assets. App is now 41 MB on first install (was 59 MB).
  • ChangedReading-mode font weight nudged down a notch. Easier on the eye in low light.
  • FixedThe "where it breaks" sidebar would sometimes render twice. It will not do that anymore.
  • FixedVoice-over now reads category names before topic titles, in the right order.
1.3.0 February 18, 2026

Connectivity, top to bottom.

Twelve new explainers covering the everyday infrastructure of getting online — and a redesigned topic header to match.

  • NewTwelve Connectivity topics — Wi-Fi, fibre, 5G, cell towers, undersea cables, GPS, Bluetooth, NFC, satellites, mesh networks, captive portals, DNS.
  • NewEach topic now opens with a one-sentence "in plain English" summary above the diagram.
  • ChangedCategory palette has been retuned for higher contrast against cream backgrounds.
  • FixedBackgrounding the app while a diagram was animating no longer freezes the next launch.
1.2.1 January 09, 2026

Corrections, with receipts.

A new "corrections" view lists every change we've made to a topic since publication, with date stamps.

  • New"Corrections" link at the bottom of each topic page. Open to see every edit, when, and why.
  • ChangedThe footnote panel now slides up from the bottom rather than pushing content sideways.
  • FixedSix minor factual tweaks following reader corrections. Listed individually under each topic's history.
1.2.0 December 12, 2025

Reading mode.

A typography-first long-read view for the topics that warrant more than a quick skim.

  • New"Reading mode" toggle on every topic — Newsreader serif, single column, no diagrams. Good on a Kindle-style e-ink iPad.
  • NewSix new Energy topics including how solar panels turn light into power and how a battery actually stores energy.
  • ChangedBumped iOS minimum to 17.0. (We can't reasonably support older versions and ship modern type rendering.)
1.1.0 November 03, 2025

Offline, by default.

Every topic now downloads on first read and stays available without a connection. The whole library fits in your pocket on a flight.

  • NewOffline reading. The full library can be pre-downloaded from Settings.
  • New"Sunday digest" — opt-in weekly notification with one new topic, hand-picked.
  • ChangedTopic count crossed 300. Now ~340 topics across seven categories.
1.0.0 September 30, 2025

Hello, world.

Public launch of How {things} Work on iOS. 250 topics across seven categories. Free forever.

  • NewInitial library — 250 topics across Home & Kitchen, Connectivity, Transport, Energy, Communication, Science, Medicine.
  • NewAnnotated diagrams, cross-sections, and the "analogy brief" footer on every topic.
  • NewSource list, footnotes, and editorial standards documentation built into every page.
  • LibraryRead it on iPhone, iPad, the Mac via Catalyst.
